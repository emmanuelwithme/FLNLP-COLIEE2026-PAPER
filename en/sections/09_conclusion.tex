\section{Conclusion}

Starting from THUIR@COLIEE 2023, this paper re-examines long-document handling, query representation, negative design, and downstream ranking integration in legal case retrieval. When the full judgment is used as the query, ModernBERT + DAPT + SFT reaches 0.1650 F1@5 on COLIEE 2026 validation, outperforming BM25 at 0.1499 and SAILER at 0.1213; citation-context queries hurt both neural models. Training quality depends mainly on negative construction: static BM25 hard negatives nearly collapse to random guessing, dynamic negative sampling stabilizes learning, and temporal scope constraints provide a smaller but legally meaningful additional gain. After LightGBM reranking and post-processing whose key component is dynamic cut-off, FLNLP reaches 0.2935 F1 on COLIEE 2026 Task~1 and ranks 7th among 22 teams. The main challenge in legal embedding-based retrieval is therefore not only model size, but also preserving full-document context, constructing informative negatives, and integrating retrieval signals under realistic hardware constraints.
